    The Obelisk

    Oh this ones great! There's about a 25 feet tall obelisk that radiates a soft blue light, it's "oily and soft like a fresh brick of parmesan cheese". The obelisk moves around, disappearing and reappearing whenever it pleases.

    It is a Night Vale tradition amongst its citizens to carve their names onto one of its four sides. Whenever it appears - the carvings disappear when the obelisk goes away and when it comes back, the ritual begins again, join us in it. Funnily enough, it makes a lot of noise, sounding "like a Bach concerto played by a French Horn and a Theremin from inside a refrigerator".

    And if it doesn't get better, it's mesmerizing existence attracts many cats, so maybe hope its around for a visit just for that.

    Or maybe you should hope it's here to be potentially chosen to have the opportunity to have any question to be answered, no matter how implausible. Be careful.

    Night Vale Community Radio

    This is where all the magic happens! -- Of course magic isn't real and you should report to the secret police if you start thinking it is -- This is the place where Cecil Palmer tells us the news and lets us know if we arw doing things right.

    It's a perfect place to come by and visit - maybe even intern - if you're a fan of our news.

    Let us give you an idea on what the place looks like!

    It's a messy home and is full of hallways winding away into a labyrinth of audio equipment and tape stacks, with windows that look out onto empty recording studios that haven't been used in decades, but that still broadcast live shows every night. Cecil also has mentioned that there are a nest of owls in the corner, like any normal radio station.

    And just in case you were worried about your safety, the station doors are carved from reclaimed bloodstone using ancient wisdom lost along with the station architects in 1942. They can only be opened by bleeding on them. And as Cecil says, "That's just part of having a good security system."
